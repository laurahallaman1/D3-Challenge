Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

Recent statistical research shows a strong correllation between the poverty and smoking. For decades, smoking has been known to be correllated with various negative health outcomes, including heart disease, lung disease, emphysema, and various cancers. With poverty now being shown to correllate with smoking, we find that the poor are disproportionately at risk for these negative health outcomes.

Based on these findings, public policy initiatives may be beneficial to help reduce smoking among the poorer members of our population, with the goal of reducing their risk of developing these chronic diseases and conditions.
